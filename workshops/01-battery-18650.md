# Workshop 01 — 18650 Battery Pack

A ~60-minute workshop for building a custom 2-cell 18650 pack to install in your future cyberdeck during Workshop 03. Triples the runtime of the default PiSugar 3, makes cells swappable, gives the deck a real "battery in there" presence.

> ⭐ **Skip this workshop if you want PiSugar 3.** Order it directly from [PiSugar](https://www.pisugar.com/) (~$35), snap it onto the back of your Pi during Workshop 03 — no soldering, no assembly.

## What you leave with

A wired and charged 2×18650 pack ready to integrate into a BBQ20 clamshell base (Workshop 02) or a slab back-cover (Workshop 03). Cells balance-charged in advance by the organizer, output verified at 5.10 V.

## Why upgrade

| Metric | PiSugar 3 (default) | 2× Samsung 30Q (this workshop) |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity | 1200 mAh @ 3.7 V | 6000 mAh @ 3.7 V (2P) |
| Runtime (Pi Zero 2 W + e-paper, light load) | ~6 hr | ~20–30 hr |
| Cells swappable | ✗ (sealed) | ✓ |
| Replacement cost when worn | ~$35 (new PiSugar) | ~$12 (two new cells) |
| Build cost | $35 (no build) | $22 (this workshop) |

Counter-intuitive but real: the 18650 path is ~$13 *cheaper* in final parts cost than the PiSugar 3 path. You pay in build time, not money.

## Cell safety briefing (mandatory, 10 min)

Non-negotiable. No skipping for late arrivals.

- 18650s are spicy. **Watch polarity. Never short.** A direct short delivers 100+ A from a 30Q — enough to weld, ignite, or vent.
- **Recommended cells:** Samsung 30Q, Samsung 35E, Molicel P28A. All have published cycle data and proper safety vents.
- **Avoid grey-market "UltraFire 9999mAh" listings.** Fake capacity, no thermal cutoff, often e-waste in 18650 sleeves.
- **Use a holder.** Never solder directly to bare cells — heat damages the chemistry. Tab-welded packs are a different workshop.
- **Cells arrive pre-charged and balance-paired.** Organizer runs every pair on a Nitecore D2 / Xtar VC2 before the workshop.
- **Store unused cells in a fire-resistant LiPo bag.**

## Wiring

```
[USB-C input]
     │
     ▼
[TP5100 BAT+/BAT-]──┬──[+] 2× 18650 in PARALLEL (2P, not 2S)
                    │
                    └──[-]
[TP5100 OUT+/OUT-]
     │
     ▼
[SPDT switch on +]
     │
     ▼
[MT3608 IN+/IN-]   <-- adjust trimmer to 5.10 V BEFORE connecting load
[MT3608 OUT+/OUT-]
     │
     ▼
[JST-PH pigtail — ready for Workshop 03 integration]
```

Critical pre-flight: multimeter on MT3608 OUT, adjust trimmer until you read 5.10 V. *Then* hand off. Boost modules ship at random voltages.

## Run-of-show (60 min)

| Time | Block |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:10 | Cell safety briefing |
| 0:10–0:30 | Solder TP5100 input/output, MT3608 input, holder leads. Heat-shrink. |
| 0:30–0:45 | Wire holder → TP5100 → switch → MT3608. **Verify MT3608 output at 5.10 V** before sign-off. |
| 0:45–0:55 | Charge test: plug USB-C in, verify TP5100 charge LED, measure pack voltage. |
| 0:55–1:00 | Label, pack, take-home tips on cell care and storage. |

## Common failure modes

- **TP5100 set to 1S instead of 2S.** Jumper config — re-check before applying power. Cells go in PARALLEL (2P), not series (2S).
- **Boost output sags under load.** MT3608 trimmer drifted, or cells mismatched. Re-trim to 5.10 V at no load; verify ≥ 5.0 V under 1 A test load.
- **One cell drains faster.** Cells weren't balance-paired — only use organizer-supplied pairs.
- **Pack doesn't fit BBQ20 base shell.** Cells are oversized (some "30Q" Amazon listings are mislabeled 18700s). Use verified-source cells only.
