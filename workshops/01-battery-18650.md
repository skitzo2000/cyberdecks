# Workshop 01 — 18650 Battery Pack Upgrade

A ~1.5-hour workshop that swaps the PiSugar 3 in your basic build for a custom 2-cell 18650 pack. Triples runtime, makes cells swappable, gives the deck a real "battery in there" presence. Prereq: [Workshop 00](./00-basic-build.md) completed (or an equivalent base build).

This upgrade adds **~$12 net** to your deck cost (the PiSugar 3 you remove goes home as a spare and isn't "lost") and lands the cumulative deck at **~$120**.

## Why upgrade

| Metric | PiSugar 3 (basic) | 2× Samsung 30Q (this workshop) |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity | 1200 mAh @ 3.7 V | 6000 mAh @ 3.7 V (2P) |
| Runtime (Pi Zero 2 W + e-paper, light load) | ~6 hr | ~20–30 hr |
| Cells swappable | ✗ (sealed) | ✓ (slide out the holder) |
| Replacement cost when worn | ~$35 (new PiSugar) | ~$12 (two new cells) |
| Build complexity | snap-on | small wired pack |

## Two paths

The workshop offers a choice on day one — declare your path when you sign up.

**Path A — Custom pack (recommended).** Build a 2P pack with a TP5100 charge module + MT3608 5V boost. More cyberpunk, you understand the circuit. ~$25/kit, more soldering.

**Path B — Drop-in UPS HAT.** Waveshare UPS HAT (B) with built-in 18650 holders, outputs 5V/5A, charges via USB-C. Plug-and-play. ~$35/kit, almost no soldering.

The run-of-show below covers Path A. Path B builders peel off after the cell-safety briefing and do an abbreviated install track in parallel.

## Cell safety briefing (mandatory, 15 min)

Non-negotiable. No skipping for late arrivals.

- 18650s are spicy. **Watch polarity. Never short.** A direct short can deliver 100+ A from a 30Q for several seconds — long enough to weld, ignite, or vent.
- **Recommended cells:** Samsung 30Q, Samsung 35E, Molicel P28A. All have published cycle data and proper safety vents.
- **Avoid grey-market "UltraFire 9999mAh" listings.** Capacity is fake (~600–1200 mAh typical), no thermal cutoff, fake cycle life. Many are e-waste 14500s in 18650 sleeves.
- **Use a holder.** Never solder directly to bare cells — the heat damages the chemistry near the anode. Tab-welded packs are the only exception (and that's a different workshop).
- **Charge cells separately first.** Use a Nitecore D2 or Xtar VC2 ($25–40) before the workshop to verify cell health and balance the pair to within 10 mV.
- **Store unused cells in a fire-resistant LiPo bag.** Cheap insurance.

The organizer brings two LiPo bags to the workshop, one for charged cells (issued to builders) and one for any failed cells (collected for proper disposal).

## Wiring (Path A)

```
[USB-C input]
     │
     ▼
[TP5100 BAT+ / BAT-]──┬──[+] 2× 18650 in parallel
                      │
                      └──[-]
[TP5100 OUT+ / OUT-]
     │
     ▼
[SPDT switch on +]
     │
     ▼
[MT3608 IN+/IN-]   <-- adjust pot to 5.1 V output BEFORE connecting Pi
[MT3608 OUT+/OUT-]
     │
     ▼
[JST-PH to Pi 5V/GND pads on bottom of board, or to the GPIO 5V pin]
```

Critical pre-flight step: connect a multimeter to MT3608 OUT and adjust the trimmer pot until you read 5.10 V. *Then* connect to the Pi. Boost modules ship at random voltages — wiring an unverified one to the Pi has cooked many a board.

## Mechanical

- Extended PETG rear shell adds ~12 mm of depth behind the basic kit's rear plane to accommodate the cells.
- 2-cell holder mounts to internal standoffs on the rear shell with M2.5 screws.
- Cells loadable through a hatch on the rear face (sliding cover with magnetic latch — STL files provided).
- Power switch protrudes through a milled slot on the right side panel.
- USB-C charge port through the left side panel.

## Software

Nothing changes. The Pi sees a regular 5 V supply — DietPi has no knowledge that the battery changed. Optionally, a fuel-gauge IC (MAX17048, ~$3) can be added later for SoC readout; not included in this workshop.

## Pre-workshop prep (organizer)

Two weeks out:
- [ ] Order cells from a trusted vendor (not Amazon Marketplace, which is flooded with counterfeits — 18650BatteryStore or LiionWholesale are reliable)
- [ ] Bulk-charge and balance every cell pair on an external charger
- [ ] Pre-set TP5100 jumpers for 2S mode on every module (a single jumper position — but get them wrong and cells overcharge)

One week out:
- [ ] Pre-set MT3608 modules to 5.10 V output. Mark with red sharpie when verified.
- [ ] Print extended rear shells and hatches.

Day before:
- [ ] Pack kits. Each bag also contains: paired cells (rubber-banded together, marked with matched serial), a LiPo storage bag.

## Common failure modes and fixes

- **Cells get warm during charge.** TP5100 not set for 2S mode (jumper config) — but actually you should be 2P here, not 2S. Re-read the wiring diagram; cells go in PARALLEL, not series. Two parallel 30Qs = 6000 mAh @ 3.7 V, not 3000 mAh @ 7.4 V. Series-wired cells without a proper 2S BMS will mis-charge and can vent. **Power off immediately, recheck wiring.**
- **Boost output sags under Pi load.** MT3608 trimmer drifted, or cells are mismatched/old. Re-trim to 5.10 V at no load, then verify it holds ≥ 5.0 V when the Pi boots.
- **One cell drains noticeably faster than the other.** Cells weren't balance-charged before assembly. Run an external balance cycle, return to service.
- **Pi reboots randomly during the screen refresh.** E-paper inrush momentarily exceeds boost capacity. Add a 470 µF electrolytic across the boost output to smooth the transient.
- **Pack doesn't fit in the extended shell.** Cells are oversized (some "30Q" listings on Amazon are 18700s mis-labelled). Replace with verified cells.
